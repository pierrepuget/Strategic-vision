The Micro-School Model

Multi-Age Learning Hub

Description & Analysis

This is what the school is already doing, directly embracing its small size by having flexible, multi-age groupings instead of strict single-grade classrooms. Learning is highly individualized, with a strong emphasis on peer mentorship.

Why it aligns with Quaker values: This model inherently builds a deep, supportive, and family-like community where everyone knows and supports each other, truly living out the Quaker testimony of Community. It allows for unparalleled individual attention, honoring each student's "Inner Light." Every student's contribution is valued, embodying Equality.

The Upsides (Pros):

  • Maximizes Resources: We can make the most of our teachers by having them work with a broader range of students.
  • Strong Community Bonds: It fosters a truly deep, supportive, and family-like community.
  • Flexible Pacing: Students aren't stuck in a rigid grade level.
  • Promotes Self-Directed Learning: Because students are often working on individualized or small-group projects, they naturally become more self-directed.

The Challenges (Cons):

  • Requires teachers who are skilled and comfortable with multi-age instruction.
  • Curriculum needs to be designed with flexible pathways.
  • Communication with parents about the benefits of multi-age learning is crucial.

Evaluation Criteria

Alignment with Quaker Values (SPICES): This model inherently builds a deep, supportive, and family-like community. It allows for unparalleled individual attention, honoring each student's "Inner Light." Every student's contribution is valued, embodying Equality.

Mission Enhancement: It advances the mission by creating a truly personalized, flexible, and community-driven learning environment, maximizing teacher expertise and fostering self-directed learning.

Market Relevance & Appeal: This model turns our small size into our biggest asset, appealing to families who want a very nurturing, personalized, and connected environment.

Operational Feasibility: This is a natural fit for the school and is already in place. It requires teachers skilled and comfortable with multi-age instruction and highly differentiated learning.

Long-Term Sustainability & Growth: This model is highly sustainable and allows for flexible pacing and individual growth, contributing to consistent enrollment numbers by appealing to a specific market.

Impact on Student Experience: It fosters strong community bonds, flexible pacing, and promotes self-directed learning, ensuring everyone feels seen and valued.

Interconnection with Other Schools: By fostering strong community bonds and flexible pacing, this model helps students develop adaptability and self-direction, which are beneficial for transitions to larger high school environments.
