The "Community as Classroom" Framework

Our Town, Our Lab

Description & Analysis

This framework takes the idea of community engagement to its extreme, making the town of State College and the surrounding areas a regular, integral part of the learning environment. Students would spend significant time learning in the community.

Why it aligns with Quaker values: This framework directly embodies the Quaker testimony of Community and Service by actively engaging students in their local environment and its needs. It also encourages honest observation and ethical engagement with real-world issues and people, reflecting Integrity. By understanding local challenges and diverse populations, students can work towards a more peaceful and equitable community.

The Upsides (Pros):

  • Authentic Learning: Provides unparalleled real-world context and relevance for academic subjects.
  • Deep Community Connection: Fosters a strong sense of belonging and civic responsibility within the local community.
  • Leverages Local Resources: Taps into the rich resources of State College without needing to build everything in-house.
  • Practical Skills: Develops skills in interviewing, observation, data collection in real settings, civic participation, and communication with diverse audiences.

The Challenges (Cons):

  • Requires significant logistical planning for transportation and supervision.
  • Building and maintaining strong, reciprocal relationships with community partners is crucial.
  • Teachers need to be skilled at designing learning experiences outside the traditional classroom.

Evaluation Criteria

Alignment with Quaker Values (SPICES): This framework directly embodies the Quaker testimony of Community and Service by actively engaging students in their local environment and its needs. It also encourages honest observation and ethical engagement with real-world issues and people, reflecting Integrity.

Mission Enhancement: It provides unparalleled real-world context and relevance for academic subjects, fostering a strong sense of belonging and civic responsibility, enhancing academic excellence and holistic development.

Market Relevance & Appeal: It offers a unique identity and a highly distinctive approach that integrates the local community into the daily learning experience, appealing to families seeking authentic, real-world learning.

Operational Feasibility: Requires significant logistical planning for transportation and supervision. Building and maintaining strong, reciprocal relationships with community partners is crucial. Teachers need to be skilled at designing learning experiences outside the traditional classroom.

Long-Term Sustainability & Growth: It leverages the rich resources of State College without needing to build everything in-house, contributing to program durability and community integration.

Impact on Student Experience: Students feel like active citizens, not just residents. It develops skills in interviewing, observation, data collection in real settings, civic participation, and communication with diverse audiences, fostering high engagement.

Interconnection with Other Schools: By making the community a classroom, this framework explicitly strengthens ties within the broader educational community and provides avenues for students to interact with diverse entities, enhancing community integration and external resource leveraging.
